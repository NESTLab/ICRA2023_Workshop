About this workshop

Intelligent multi-robot systems are increasingly receiving interest from academia and industry. They promise versatility and ability to adapt to diverse scenarios with dangerous and dynamic environments.

However, achieving robust and resilient autonomy in the real world is currently an open problem even for individual robots. Multi-robot systems pose a vast array of additional issues, which include coherence in state estimation, task coordination, and security in communication.

This workshop aims to bring together researchers and practitioners to discuss the challenges of experimental validation and sim-to-real transfer of multi-agent coordination algorithms, as well as favored solutions and future directions. The ultimate objective is to acquire a better understanding of the technological gaps that hinder immediate and broad deployment of multi-robot systems for use in real-world scenarios, such as package delivery, warehouse management, exploration, firefighting, and more.
